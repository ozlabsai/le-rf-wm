Five More Collapse Mechanisms

1

BatchNorm → LayerNorm

BN running stats diverge between train/eval with random-init encoders. LN has no running stats.

2

Overlapping slices → Non-overlapping context/target

Original LeWM uses overlapping slices (works for action-conditioned). For unconditional: predictor just copies the overlap. Trivial shortcut.

3

Absolute prediction → Residual prediction (Δz)

Predicting z directly allows memorizing scene identity. Predicting Δz = zt+k - zt forces learning temporal dynamics.

4

Raw MSE → L2-normalized MSE

Raw MSE allows magnitude shrinkage: predict near-zero vectors, minimize loss trivially. L2-norm forces directional accuracy.

5

Variance loss NaN → sqrt(var + eps) hinge

VICReg variance regularizer hit NaN at var=0 due to sqrt(0). Added eps and hinge loss to stabilize.

Each fix addresses a specific collapse or shortcut mechanism. All were necessary.
