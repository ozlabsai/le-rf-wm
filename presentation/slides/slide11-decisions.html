Key Design Decisions

Mean pooling over CLS token

CLS has zero variance at random init with untrained encoder

LayerNorm over BatchNorm

BN running stats diverge between train/eval modes

Residual prediction

Predicting delta_z prevents scene-identity memorization shortcuts

L2-normalized loss

Prevents magnitude shrinkage shortcut where predictor outputs near-zero

Non-overlapping slices

Context/target windows don't overlap. Prevents trivial copy shortcuts.

Rollout-aware training

Multi-step loss teaches temporal composition, not just one-step accuracy.

Every decision addresses a specific failure mode discovered during development.
